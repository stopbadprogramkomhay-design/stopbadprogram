Bullying and Discrimination

What is Bullying?

Bullying is repeated aggressive behavior, intentional harm, or intimidation towards someone perceived as vulnerable. It can be physical, verbal, or social.

Ang pambubully ay paulit-ulit na agresibong pag-uugali, sinadyang pananakit, o pananakot sa isang tao na itinuturing na mahina. Maaari itong pisikal, berbal, o sosyal.

What is Discrimination?

Discrimination is the unfair or prejudicial treatment of individuals based on characteristics such as race, gender, age, religion, or disability. It often denies people equal opportunities or rights.

Ang diskriminasyon ay hindi patas o mapanuring pagtrato sa mga indibidwal batay sa katangian tulad ng lahi, kasarian, edad, relihiyon, o kapansanan. Madalas nitong ipinagkakait ang pantay na oportunidad o karapatan sa tao.

7 Actionable Framework

S

Seek Support: Find a safe adult, a teacher, or a counselor immediately.

Kalungkutan: Madalas malungkot o tahimik.

T

Tell the Truth: Report the specific details of the incident via the Guidance Office.

Problema sa paaralan: Hindi pumapasok o mababang grado.

O

Observe & Own: Note the facts, the who, what, and where; take ownership of the decision to stand up/report.

Sobrang iritasyon: Biglaang galit o iyak.

P

Protect Yourself/Others: Move away from the situation and use safe coping skills.

Mga marka: Hindi maipaliwanag na pasa.

B

Be an Upstander: Intervene safely by distracting, calling for help, or supporting the victim.

Pag-iisa: Iniiwasan ang kaibigan.

A

Access Resources: Use the S.T.O.P. B.A.D. website and hotline numbers for help.

Pag-aalala: Takot sa ilang tao.

D

Do Not Engage: Do not retaliate, argue, or share rumors/online posts about the incident.

Mababang tiwala: Panghuhusga sa sarili.
